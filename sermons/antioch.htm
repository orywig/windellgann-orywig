The Great Church At Antioch

Acts 11:19-30; 13:1-3

Introduction

1. From Jerusalem the gospel spread to Cyprus & Cyrene, v. 19.

To Antioch, 3rd largest city in the Empire.

Opposition / persecution scattered the Jerusalem church.

2. To the Jews only -

Then to the Grecians- following Cornelius conversion by Peter in Acts 10-11.

3. It must have been A UNITED CHURCH though mixed in its ethnic make-up.

a. Jews - v. 19

b. Grecians - v.20

c. cf. Acts 13:1

 

1. CHRIST WAS PREACHED -

"Jesus as Lord" - v. 20 (b), v. 21

1. The same was preached in Samaria - Acts 8:5

a. Acts 8:5

b. Acts 8:12 "kingdom" and "name of Christ"

2. To the Ethiopian - Acts 8:35

a. A capsule of what preaching "Christ" involves is given by Peter in Acts 10:36-43.

3. Now at Antioch - v. 20 (b) and v. 21

 

2. THE LOST WERE CONVERTED

- It was a growing church. - v. 21

1. Many "believed and turned" - we know from Acts 8 what it meant to be coverted to Christ.

Believing and turning included repentance and being baptized - Acts 2:38; Acts 8:

 

3. MEMBERS WERE EXHORTED - v. 23

1. Barnabas was sent to the Antioch church from the apostles in Jerusalem. V. 22ff.

A. His very name mean "Exhorter" or "Encourager" - a play on his name.

2. They enjoyed God's grace -- v. 23

3. They cleaved to the Lord -

4. They assembled faithfully - v. 26

A. Along with Barnabas and Saul.

5. Much people were being taught - converted and converts growing. (2Pet 3:18)

6. The Disciples were called "Christians" first at Antioch - v. 26.

A The term "Called" { khray-mat-id'-zo} indicates the term was given by divine oracle.

B. Isaiah 62:2; Eph. 3:15

 

4. A BENEVOLENT CHURCH - v. 28 - 29

1. Agabus was an inspired prophet.

2. All the members participated, "every man" - v. 29

3. They gave liberally & proportionally - "each according to his ability" - v. 29

4. They were a concerned and caring people - v. 29 (b)

5. They organized and cooperated to complete this benevolence - v. 30.

Church to Barnabas and Saul - to the elders of churches in Judea.

 

5. A MISSION MINDED CHURCH - Acts 13:1-3

1. Prophets and Teachers in the church ministered to the Lord.

Preaching and teaching the Lord's Word.

2. "Fasted" and prayed - v. 3.

Sent out Barnabas and Paul - "missionary" or "ambassadors" "apostles" of the Antioch church -

Conclusion:

1. We should want the church today to be like this great church at Antioch.

2. We should want to be a part of it.
