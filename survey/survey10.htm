	SELECT CORRECT ANSWER(S) 
 




_____1. 	The captive princes were nourished and trained: 1. one; 2. two; 3. three; 4. four; years before standing before the king. (ch.1)

 

_____2. 	Daniel wanted Melzar, the stewart, to give him pulse (vegetables) and water on a trial basis of: 1. five; 2. ten; 3. fifteen; 4. twenty days.



_____3. 	When the king enquired of the Hebrews, he found them: 1. twice, 2. three times; 3. five times; 4. ten times, better than all the magicians and astrologers.



_____4.	 Because the wise men could not interpret his dream, Nebuchadnezzar ordered them: 1. imprisoned; 2. exiled; 3. destroyed.



_____5. 	As a reward Nebuchadnezzar: 1. gave Daniel high honors; 2. gave him gifts; 3. made him ruler over the whole province of Babylon; 4. made him chief over all the wise men of Babylon.



_____6.	 Following the three Hebrew's deliverance from the fiery furnace, N. made a decree: 1. No people, nation, language was to speak against God of Shadrack, M. & A.; 2. If they did, they would be cut in pieces; 3. If they did their house would be destroyed; 4. Because no other God can deliver like this.



_____7. 	Nebuchadnezzar often: 1. praised God; 2. had meaningful dreams; 3. felt jealous of Daniel.




_____8. 	To bring him to confess God's might, God afflicted Nebuchadnezzar with: 1. leprosy; 2. turning him into a wild man; 3. by taking his sight.




_____9. 	Belshazzar: 1. made a great feast; 2. was never fearful; 3. was last of Babylonian kings.




____10. 	Once a Medo-persian king issued a decree (6:8,15): 1. it took 4/5 of parliament to change it; 2. it took a year to revoke it; 3. Even the king could not alter or retract it.

Note: 	Daniel was taken captive as a young boy at the beginning of the Babylonian captivity even before Jerusalem was destroyed.  He remained until Cyrus the Medo-Persian king came to the throne (he allowed the Israelites to return).  Daniel occupied positions of authority in both governments and probably never saw his home again.  He remained faithful to God.
